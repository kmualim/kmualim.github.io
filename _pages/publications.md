      <h2>Research &amp; Publications</h2>

      <div class="pub-list">

        <div class="pub-item">
          <img src="/images/genetic_diversity.png" alt="Genetic diversity trajectories">
          <div class="pub-body">
            <h3><a href="http://kmualim.github.io/publications/2020-05-01-abc">Predicting genetic diversity trajectories in the Anthropocene</a></h3>
            <p>
              Genetic diversity is crucial for species' ability to adapt and has been targeted for protection in the UN's Global Biodiversity Framework.
              Using genetic data from 18 species, we found that genetic diversity declines slowly after habitat loss but continues for decades even after habitats are protected.
              Our model projects significant genetic diversity losses across 13,808 species, with short-term losses of 13–22% and long-term losses of 42–48%.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="pub-item">
          <img src="/images/encode.png" alt="ENCODE encyclopedia">
          <div class="pub-body">
            <h3><a href="https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.11.09.563812v1">An encyclopedia of enhancer–gene regulatory interactions in the human genome</a></h3>
            <p>
              We developed a resource of over 13 million enhancer–gene interactions across 352 cell types and tissues.
              Using CRISPR experiments, chromatin measurements, and genetic studies, we built ENCODE-rE2G — a model that
              predicts how enhancers regulate target genes and links genetic variants to disease across the genome.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="pub-item">
          <img src="/images/mar.png" alt="Mutations-area relationship">
          <div class="pub-body">
            <h3><a href="https://ecoevorxiv.org/repository/view/4793/">Power and limitations of the mutations-area relationship (MAR) for genetic diversity targets</a></h3>
            <p>
              An evaluation of the MAR framework for predicting a species' loss of genetic diversity given habitat loss —
              analogous to the species-area relationship used in conservation. We discuss its power, limitations, and
              improvements to guide practitioners meeting UN post-2020 Sustainable Development Goals.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="pub-item">
          <img src="/images/coal.png" alt="Population divergence">
          <div class="pub-body">
            <h3><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41437-021-00435-8">An analytical solution to estimating population divergence</a></h3>
            <p>
              We developed the G(A|B) method to estimate when populations split from their common ancestors using DNA sequences,
              without requiring assumptions about population size history. We demonstrate the method on Neanderthal and Denisovan
              genomes and show it can test whether population history fits a tree topology across three or more groups.
              <em>Heredity</em>, 2021.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="pub-item">
          <img src="/images/ppif.png" alt="Disease risk variants">
          <div class="pub-body">
            <h3><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-03446-x">Linking risk variants to disease genes</a></h3>
            <p>
              Using the Activity-by-Contact (ABC) Model, we created enhancer maps for 131 cell types and tissues, connecting
              over 5,000 genetic signals to more than 2,000 genes across 72 diseases and traits.
              <em>Nature</em>, 2021.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>

      </div>
